Quiz Copy Explainer

The strategy, psychology, and reasoning behind every copy decision in the New Vintage Handbags quiz funnel

1. Question Rationale ▼
Question 1
Type: Card Selection | Diagnostic: desired_outcome
What draws you to a handbag first?
Why this question: Opens the quiz with an identity-level question. Instead of asking about budget or preferences directly, it asks what they VALUE in a handbag. This immediately frames the quiz as a personality exploration, not a shopping quiz. It also serves as the primary diagnostic question for email personalization, since each answer reveals a fundamentally different motivation (aesthetics vs. craftsmanship vs. meaning vs. exclusivity).
Card selection format: Visual cards with icons create an engaging first impression. The descriptions under each answer help users identify with the psychology, not just the surface answer.
Question 2
Type: Scale Slider
How adventurous are you with your accessories?
Why this question: Directly measures the boldness spectrum, which is the primary differentiator between profiles. A slider feels natural for a spectrum answer and prevents the "right answer" bias that multiple choice can create. Users are more honest when they can place themselves on a continuum.
Slider labels: "I keep it classic" to "The bolder, the better" uses approachable language instead of numeric scales. Both ends feel valid and aspirational.
Question 3
Type: Tag Cloud (multi-select) | Diagnostic: current_situation
Which occasions would you carry a painted bag?
Why this question: Reveals lifestyle context and purchase intent. Someone who selects "everyday errands" has different needs than "weddings & events." The multi-select format captures nuance. This is the second diagnostic question because it tells us HOW the person will use the product, which directly informs email content recommendations.
Tag cloud format: Pill-style tags feel light and fun. Multi-select generates rich data for personalization without making the user choose just one answer.
Question 4
Type: Emoji Scale
How do you feel about your current handbag collection?
Why this question: Gauges dissatisfaction with current collection (high dissatisfaction = higher purchase intent). The emoji format makes an potentially sensitive question feel playful and safe. The helper text "Be honest. We won't judge!" lowers the guard. This question directly correlates with temperature scoring, since someone who is "frustrated" or "just getting started" is in a different buying state than someone who is "obsessed."
Question 5
Type: Multiple Choice | Diagnostic: buying_signal
A friend compliments your bag. What do you most want them to say?
Why this question: This is the most psychologically revealing question in the quiz. It asks what EMOTIONAL PAYOFF the user seeks from their accessories. "Where did you get that?!" = social currency seeker. "Most gorgeous bag" = aesthetics-driven. "That's actual art" = craft appreciator. "Need one for my mom" = gift-oriented. Each answer maps directly to how we position products in follow-up emails.
Framing as a friend's compliment: Projection technique. Asking what compliment they want is more revealing than asking what they value, because it taps into aspirational identity.
Question 6
Type: Yes/No Toggle
Would you consider having a custom design created just for you?
Why this question: Direct buying signal. "Yes" answers score 16 points and heavily tag toward the personalization spectrum. This question also informs the CTA strategy: custom-interested users get pushed toward the consultation process, while catalog browsers get pushed toward ready-to-ship pieces. The toggle format makes it feel low-commitment while capturing high-value intent data.
Question 7
Type: Star Rating
How important is owning something truly one-of-a-kind?
Why this question: Final question captures the core value proposition alignment. NVH's entire business is built on uniqueness. Someone who rates this 5 stars is deeply aligned with the brand. 1 star means they need more education on why one-of-one matters. This question has the widest score spread (4-16 points) making it highly influential on the final temperature reading.
Star rating format: Stars feel familiar and fast. Perfect for the final question when the user is ready to see results. The labels ("nice to have" to "non-negotiable") add personality.

3. Scoring Logic Explained ▼

How Temperature Scoring Works

Each answer assigns a point value (0-18). Points accumulate across all 7 questions toward a maximum of 100. Higher scores indicate higher purchase intent and buying readiness.

The score determines TEMPERATURE (email sequence routing). The PROFILE is determined separately by tag accumulation. A user can be a Cold Statement Maker or a Hot Aspiring Luxurist. Temperature and profile are independent.

4. Email Sequence Strategy ▼

Layered Personalization Architecture

Every quiz lead receives emails personalized on three layers:

Layer 1: Temperature (Sequence Selection)
Hot leads get direct CTAs and urgency. Cold leads get education and social proof. This ensures no lead feels pressured or neglected.
Layer 2: Profile (Content Blocks)
10 emails contain {{profile_block}} placeholders. Each resolves to profile-specific copy (50 total blocks). A Statement Maker sees different pain points, stories, and visions than a Sentimental Gifter.
Layer 3: Quiz Answers (Answer Callbacks)
7 emails contain {{answer_callback}} placeholders. These reference specific quiz answers naturally: "You mentioned you'd carry a painted bag for everyday errands..." This creates a "they really listened" feeling.

Sequence Design Philosophy

Welcome (All Leads, Days 0-5)
Delivers results, reveals a "blindspot" insight, shares an actionable tip, and introduces the brand story. Every lead gets this regardless of temperature. Goal: establish trust and demonstrate value.
Cold Nurture (Days 1-23)
7-email education sequence. Teaches what painted bags are, how they're made, debunks myths, shares customer stories, and paints a vision of transformation. Ends with a soft check-in. Never pushy. Goal: move from awareness to consideration.
Warm Activation (Days 1-15)
6-email conversion sequence. Opens with personalized answer reference, shares a case study, handles the #1 objection (mock-up process), compiles social proof, presents three purchase paths, and closes with a before/after comparison. Goal: remove barriers and inspire action.
Hot Path (Days 1-9)
4-email fast-track. Direct CTA with curated picks, speed-focused case study, scarcity/urgency nudge, and final emotional push. These leads already want to buy. Goal: reduce friction and create urgency.
Re-Engagement (Post-Sequence)
3-email win-back. Sent after the temperature sequence ends. Casual check-in, fresh content/new arrivals, and a warm farewell with Instagram redirect. Goal: capture late converters and maintain brand connection.
5. Persuasion Techniques Used ▼
Identity Labeling
Assigning users a personality label ("You're The Statement Maker") creates an identity they want to live up to. People are more likely to take actions consistent with their self-image. The profile name becomes an aspirational identity, not just a quiz result.
Scarcity (Real)
Every NVH bag IS genuinely one-of-a-kind. This isn't manufactured urgency. The copy leans into this authentic scarcity: "Once it's gone, it's gone forever." Real scarcity is more persuasive than artificial countdown timers.
Social Proof Stacking
Celebrity clients (Miranda Lambert, Megan Fox), press features (People, Marie Claire), review count (200+), Instagram following (100K+), and specific customer testimonials are layered throughout. Each type of social proof reinforces a different trust factor.
Risk Reversal
The digital mock-up process is the key objection handler. "See exactly what your bag will look like before we paint a single brushstroke." This converts the fear of commitment into excitement about collaboration.
The Blindspot Reveal
WELCOME-01 mentions a "style blindspot." This creates curiosity and positions the brand as insightful. It also gives each profile a growth edge that makes the results feel personalized and nuanced, not just flattering.
Projection Technique (Q5)
Asking "What compliment do you want to hear?" uses projection to reveal the emotional payoff they seek. This is more revealing than asking directly what they value, because it taps into aspirational self-image.
Progressive Commitment
The quiz starts with easy identity questions and builds to higher-commitment questions (custom interest, importance of uniqueness). By Q6-Q7, users are invested in the outcome and more likely to provide their email.
Anti-Bagflation Positioning
The copy subtly positions NVH as the "smart luxury" alternative to declining mainstream brands. "People are tired of paying more for declining quality." This taps into a real consumer frustration validated by market data.
